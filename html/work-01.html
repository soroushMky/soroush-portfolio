RUDSAK — Outerwear Campaign

Leading graphic design and product positioning for one of Canada's leading outerwear brands, across digital and physical touchpoints.

ClientRUDSAK
RoleGraphic Artist & Product Designer
DurationMay – Dec 2023 · 8 months
ToolsFigma · Adobe Suite
01 Research

RUDSAK needed cohesive seasonal visual assets across digital and physical touchpoints — but campaigns were being produced ad-hoc, with no unified system tying them together.

I conducted customer and stakeholder research to identify pain points across the existing visual library, analyzing market trends among comparable outerwear brands to understand where RUDSAK's brand expression was falling short of competitors.

Key finding: imagery across channels felt disconnected — e-commerce banners, in-store signage, and social content all spoke different visual languages.

Early moodboard exploration — texture, tone, and seasonal palette direction
02 Process & Iterations

Rather than designing in isolation, I built a flexible visual system iteratively — testing each component against real product photography before locking the direction.

Iteration 01
First pass leaned heavily on bold typography overlays — tested well for impact but clashed with RUDSAK's quieter, premium brand voice. Scrapped in favor of more restrained layouts.
Iteration 02
Shifted to image-led compositions with minimal text — letting the outerwear's texture and craftsmanship carry the visual weight. Stakeholders responded strongly to this direction.
Final Direction
Combined the image-led approach with a consistent grid system and typography scale — applied across banners, product imagery, and UI components for full consistency.
03 Solution

I designed the outerwear category's complete digital asset library — product imagery, campaign banners, and reusable UI components for the e-commerce platform.

Throughout, I collaborated cross-functionally with marketing and product teams to ensure every asset stayed consistent with the system, regardless of who was producing the next piece of content.

05 Learnings
01
Restraint often reads as premium — the simplest direction outperformed the boldest one when tested with stakeholders.
02
Building a system (not just assets) meant other teams could extend the work themselves without losing consistency.
03
Early stakeholder research saved significant rework later — the disconnect was clear before a single asset was made.
